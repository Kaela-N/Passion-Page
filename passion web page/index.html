Other Flowers

Hellebores

  • Also known as Christmas roses because they bloom in the later winter (but are actually part of the buttercup family!)
  • Symbolizes serenity, tranquility, and peace
  • Hellebores are associated with a variety of folklore and symbolic associations depending on the culture; some of those associations range from protecting and warding against evil to representing rebirth and new beginnings

Hydrangeas

  • Also called "Ajisai" in Japan, these flowers bloom in rainy seasons or from June to July
  • Blue hydrangeas symbolize apology, gratitude, and understanding. White hydrangeas can symbolize boasting or purity and grace, pinks symbolize romance and sincerity
  • First originating in Japan, there goes a legend that a Japanese emperor gifted them to his lover to apolgoize for neglecting her, leaving a long lasting impression on her family and entire empire

Alstroemerias

  • Also known as the Peruvian Lily or Lily of the Incas
  • Often known to symbolize mutual support,they cna also symbolize friendship, love, strength, and devotion
  • Alstromerias were named by a biologist named Carl Linneaus after his friend Claus von Alstroemer who collected the seeds while on a botanical exploration in South America

Tulips

  • A flower that comes in a variety of colors and is invaluable to history because of how it has been praised for its beauty
  • Symbolizes perfect, deep, unconditional love
  • Originally a wildflower from Central Asia, these flowers symbolized the coming of Spring and have become popular nearly everywhere in the world for their beauty

Lillies

  • Also known as "Liliums," there are many types of lilies and types of stories and meanings to go along with them
  • Symbolizes purity,love, renewal, and transience
  • Lilies are the national flower of Italy and also have roots in Greek Mythology in addition to other folklore. In Greek Mythology, lilies are said to be flowers that Hera, Queen of Heaven and Godess of Love and Marriage, created fields of on Earth

Baby's Breath

  • Also called Gypsophila, the flowers are popular for any occasion and often given to new mothers for good luck
  • Symbolizes devotion to a relationship, a reminder to cherish each other and show as much affection as possible, everlasting love,and purity
  • The name "Baby's Breath" may come from the fragility and delicay of the flower, relating back to the popular meanings associated with the flower
